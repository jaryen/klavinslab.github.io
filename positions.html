Postdoctoral Scholar

The Klavins lab has postdoctoral scholar positions open in synthetic biology for highly motivated, independent researchers. We are interested in candidates with strong analytical and computational skills who ideally have experience in synthetic biology, molecular biology, microbiology, plant biology, or a similar area. Our research includes engineered gene networks and synthetic multicellular systems applied to wide range of problems from cell-based diagnostics to biofuel production. Our interdisciplinary group takes a primarily engineering approach to all aspects of synthetic biology, from exploring the computational expressibility of biological pathways to developing software to control laboratory processes. We collaborate with plant biologists, evolutionary biologists, computer engineers, and control systems engineers.

Graduate Student Research Assistant

The Klavins lab is always seeking highly motivated, independent graduate students in computer engineering, bioengineering, and biology for a variety of projects ranging from building gene networks that compute to developing an automated research laboratory.

Programmer

The Klavins lab has a position open for a computer programmer to work on the Aquarium project. Prior experience in web development with MySQL, Ruby/Rails, and jQuery is required. The ideal candidate is also an expert in one or more of the following: programming language design, user interface design, embedded system programming, or laboratory management systems.

System Adminimstrator

The Klavins lab is seeking a part time system administrator. Duties include managing windows and linux machines, installing and updating software, purchasing and installing computers, evaluating and installing new software, developing scripts, managing data, developing and managing backup systems, and managing systems that control scientific equipment.

Undergraduate Laboratory Assistant

The Klavins Lab is seeking UW undergraduate laboratory assistants to support experimental activities in systems and synthetic biology with Aquarium. Duties include preparing media, pouring plates, molecular cloning, collecting data, monitoring experiments, cleaning and sterilizing equipment and glassware, and general laboratory maintenance. Our lab uses a sophisticated computer system to manage samples and protocols. Training will be provided. Strong computer skills and at least one laboratory class in biochemistry or related discipline are required. We are looking for at least a one-year commitment. Sophomores and juniors are ideal.
